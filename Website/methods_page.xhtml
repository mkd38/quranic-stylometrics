Rhetorical Devices Studied

  • Amplification

    الله or Allah is the Arabic term for "God", literally. It is not a word exclusive to Muslims, as Arab Christians and Jews also refer to God as Allah. In Islam, it is believed that God has many names. Thus, the Qur'an often refers to God by descriptions of God's attributes. These are referred to as the "99 names of God" in Islam and we decided to tag them as Amplification in our XML markup. Amplification is used in order to call attention to certain points; in this case, the attributes of God.

  • Emphasis

    While comparing the English translations to the Qur'an, we noticed that the authors sometimes used words like "verily", "indeed" and "lo" as well exclamation marks "!" to emphasise certain sentences, while these words were not used in Arabic in the Qur'an. Thus, we decided to tag them to see to as if their usage in English translation was corelated to anything in the Qur'an.

  • Irony

    Irony is a rhetorical device in which a statement or situation contrasts with what is expected or known, and is thus senstive to social contexts and norms as well as linguistic differences across languages.

  • Imagery

    Imagery is a literary device that uses vivid description to appeals to a readers' senses to create an image or idea in their head.

Processing

The Qur'an is organized by chapters and verses, henceforth surahs and ayahs, respectively. Due to the constraints of this project, the team members selected three surahs for analysis. These surahs were selected subjectively based on cultural significance and richness of the text. Our were texts sourced from the Perseus Digital Library under Creative Commons licensing. Using XML, the texts were annotated to tag instances of eleven different stylistic devices.
